Seeing as discussions around school re-openings are highly controversial, we are interested in analyzing the risk associated with bringing students back to the classroom.

MATLAB was used to parse Wikipedia entries to obtain data on COVID-19 cases in various US states. Based on data availability, we looked at the initial infection date of a state and follow the new cases and deaths for 100 days. Knowing this information, a SIR model is used to compute biologic parameters of the virus, such as the basic reproduction number, and average contact frequency. Next, we looked at the demographic breakdown of several key universities to see which states the students would be coming from in the fall. Finally, a machine learning model is deployed to make a prediction on the number of new cases from the influx of students.
